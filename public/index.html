Executive Summary

This report establishes the regulatory and operational compliance framework applicable to Halliburton and Tejas operations in Brazil, and provides evidence-based justification for identified risk exposure, grounded entirely in publicly available open data from official Brazilian government sources.

The analysis draws on the ANP's SISO-Incidentes database — 30,054 registered incidents from 2013 to 2026, published under Brazil's Freedom of Information Law (Lei nº 12.527/2011) — cross-referenced with Bureau Veritas classification rules and applicable Brazilian regulatory instruments.

Key Findings
+39,000% increase in CSB barrier element failures from 1 incident in 2016 to 391 in 2025 — constituting the dominant well integrity risk category in Brazilian E&P.
193 primary barrier loss events (kicks) recorded between 2013 and 2026 on ANP-licensed installations.
2,291 critical well integrity incidents in total are documented in the open dataset.
Halliburton's well services and Tejas's completion/flow-control equipment operate within the barrier systems captured by these statistics, making compliance with Resolução ANP nº 46/2016 and BV NR 445 directly applicable.

CSB Barrier Element Failures

Falha de elemento do Conjunto Solidário de Barreira. HAL provides BOP, wellhead, and completion barrier elements directly within this classification.

Primary Barrier Loss (Kicks)
—
Falha da barreira primária (kick). Influx of formation fluid — directly linked to mud weight management and BOP response systems supplied by Halliburton.
ANP Res. 46/2016 · BV NR 459

Structural Well Failures
—
Falha estrutural em poço. Casing, tubing, and liner failures — Tejas specializes in flow control and completion equipment within this failure mode.
BV NR 445 · NR-37
💥
Loss of Well Control
—
Perda de controle de poço (menor, significante, maior). The most severe category — involves complete failure of both primary and secondary barrier systems.
ANP Res. 46/2016 · NORMAM-01
