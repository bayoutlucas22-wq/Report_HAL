# CIS Intelligence System — Regional Logic & Incident Focus

This document correlates the analytical logic for the three core regions within the HAL Intelligence Dashboard. Each country serves a distinct purpose and focuses on specific risk domains and incident typologies.

## 🇧🇷 Brazil (ANP)
**Core Focus:** Well Completions, Safety Valves, & Offshore Well Integrity
**Primary Driver:** Problems arising in completions operations.
- **Data Source:** ANP (Agência Nacional do Petróleo), SGIP/SGSO incident reports.
- **Context:** While operations span multiple domains, **for the scope of this intelligence study**, the focus is strictly on deepwater pre-salt completions. The regulatory reporting in Brazil is highly transparent, meaning every component failure (e.g., DHSV malfunction, packer leaks, BOP failures) is strictly documented and publicly retrievable via ANP databases.
- **Key Risks:** Completion hardware integrity (Domain 4), kick/blowout incidents during intervention, and compliance with ANP Res. 46/2016.

## 🇦🇷 Argentina (SESCO)
**Core Focus:** Unconventional Hydraulic Fracturing (Fracking Curve)
**Primary Driver:** The explosive growth of the fracking curve in Vaca Muerta.
- **Data Source:** SESCO (Secretaría de Energía), Adjunto IV risk records.
- **Context:** The Vaca Muerta basin is driving intense operational scaling. HAL's exposure here is hyper-focused on high-pressure stimulation, immense proppant volumes, and cementing deep lateral lengths.
- **Key Risks:** Fracking operations (Domain 1) causing casing structural stress, H₂S exposure, ultra-high PSI (11,000+) blowouts. The compliance framework revolves around Res. SE 25/2004 and managing the logistical scale-up of stage counts.

## 🇲🇽 Mexico (SIH / CNH)
**Core Focus:** Complex Shallow-Water Offshore & HPHT Carbonate Drilling
**Primary Driver:** Complex, naturally fractured carbonate drilling and directional challenges.
- **Data Source:** CNH (Comisión Nacional de Hidrocarburos), SIH Perforación.
- **Context:** Dominated by PEMEX in the Sureste and Burgos basins, Mexico requires extensive directional drilling, MWD/LWD, and robust cementing in challenging offshore HPHT (High Pressure, High Temperature) environments. Transparency exists but is fragmented compared to Brazil.
- **Key Risks:** Offshore well control incidents (Domain 2 & 3), kick prevention in naturally fractured carbonates, and strict adherence to CNH drilling guidelines and NOM-115 standards.

---
**Summary:**
While all three regions conform to the same structured reporting standard (Audit, Incident Registry, Cross-Analysis), their underlying data queries and risk matrices pivot around their unique operational realities: **Brazil = Completions**, **Argentina = Fracking**, and **Mexico = Directional/Offshore Drilling**.
